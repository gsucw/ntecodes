Neverness to Everness Guide

NTE Guide — Tips, Banners, and Beginner Guide

Everything you need to know about Neverness to Everness: gacha banners, currency systems, character progression, team composition, and how to get the most out of your early game experience.

01 Game Overview

Neverness to Everness (NTE) is a free-to-play open-world anime RPG developed by Hotta Studio (creators of Tower of Fantasy) and published by Perfect World. The game launched globally on April 29, 2026 on PC, iOS, and Android with full cross-platform save support.

The game is set in Hethereau, a sprawling modern city where supernatural distortions known as Anomalies have begun bleeding into reality. Players assume the role of an Appraiser — a unique individual capable of sensing and analyzing these Anomalies. Alongside a team of Anomaly Hunters, you explore the city, unravel its mysteries, and fight back against the growing supernatural threat.

Combat is real-time action-based with elemental interactions, dodge mechanics, and team-based combo attacks. The open world offers vertical exploration through parkour, gliding, and rooftop traversal, with hidden chests, puzzles, and lore collectibles scattered throughout Hethereau.

02 Currency System

NTE uses several currencies, each with a specific purpose:

  • Annulith — The premium gacha currency. Used to pull on character and weapon banners. Earned through exploration, events, battle pass, and gift codes. 1 pull = 100 Annulith. 10-pull = 1,000 Annulith.
  • Beetle Coins — The standard shop currency. Used to buy items in the in-game shop, including upgrade materials, character fragments, and consumables. Earned through gameplay, events, and codes.
  • Fons — Basic in-game currency used for standard purchases, upgrading low-tier equipment, and town services. Easily earned through quests and exploration.
  • Star Crystals — A premium top-up currency purchased with real money, convertible 1:1 to Annulith. Buying through the shop also grants a one-time bonus at each tier.

For free-to-play players, the most efficient strategy is to save Annulith for limited character banners, spend Beetle Coins on refreshable weekly upgrade materials, and redeem every active gift code for bonus resources.

03 Banner & Gacha System

The gacha system in NTE follows a standard model familiar to anime RPG players, with a few unique twists:

Character Event Banners — Limited-time banners featuring a rate-up 5-star character. 50/50 system: if you pull a 5-star, there's a 50% chance it's the featured character. If you lose the 50/50, the next 5-star is guaranteed to be the featured character. Pity carries over between banners. Soft pity begins around pull 75, with hard pity at 90.

Weapon Banner — Limited weapon banners with similar pity mechanics but lower pity thresholds (hard pity at 80). Fate points ensure you can eventually target a specific weapon.

Standard Banner — Permanent banner with a rotating pool of standard characters and weapons. Uses a different currency (Standard Pull Tickets earned through gameplay). No 50/50 mechanic — you have equal chance at any standard 5-star.

Beginner Banner — A discounted banner for new players where your first 10-pull costs 50% less Annulith and guarantees at least one 4-star character. Highly recommended as your first pull.

Pro tips for banner strategy: Never spend Annulith on the Standard Banner — save all premium currency for limited character banners. Plan ahead by checking upcoming banner leaks and official announcements. If you're free-to-play, commit to saving for one or two key characters rather than pulling randomly.

04 Character Progression Guide

Characters in NTE level up through multiple systems. Here's what to focus on at each stage:

Character Level — Use Hunter Guides (Rising, Senior, Elite tiers) to level up characters. Higher-tier Guides give more XP per material. Early game: level your main DPS to 50 first, then support and healer to 40. Mid game: push all three active team members to 60 or higher.

Weapons — Each character equips a weapon that provides base stats and a passive ability. Weapons can be leveled with upgrade ore and refined (duplicate copies increase the passive effect). Focus on maxing one weapon for your main DPS before spreading resources.

Artifacts/Echoes — Equipment slots that provide stat bonuses and set effects. The endgame grind in NTE revolves around farming optimal Echo sets from Domain challenges. Early on, use any 4-star or 5-star Echoes with the right main stats. Don't farm Echoes extensively until you reach the highest Domain tier (typically Adventure Level 40+).

Talent/Ascension Materials — Characters require specific materials to ascend past level caps and unlock new talents. Weekly bosses provide the rarest talent materials (limited to 3 drops per week). Plan your weekly boss runs around the characters you're actively building.

Constellations — Duplicate character pulls grant Constellation upgrades (similar to C1-C6 in other games). Each constellation unlocks a new passive bonus. For 4-star characters, constellations can make a significant difference. For 5-star characters, C0 (base) is usually viable for all content, with C2 and C6 being major power spikes.

05 Team Building Tips

NTE teams consist of three active party members with the option to swap between them during combat. Effective team building focuses on elemental reactions and role coverage:

  • Standard Team Structure: 1 Main DPS + 1 Sub-DPS/Support + 1 Healer/Shielder. The main DPS stays on-field the most; supports provide off-field damage, buffs, or healing.
  • Elemental Reactions: NTE has an elemental system with reactions triggered by applying two elements in sequence. Pair elements that synergize well — for example, Fire + Electric creates an Overload explosion, while Ice + Water freezes enemies.
  • Weapon Variety: Different weapon types (swords, guns, fists, katanas) have unique playstyles. Build a team that can handle different combat scenarios — close-range, ranged, and AoE.
  • Flexible Teammates: The Abyssal Tower (endgame mode) requires two separate teams, so invest in at least 6-8 characters over time.

06 Daily & Weekly Routine

To maximize your resource income in NTE, follow this routine:

Daily (10-15 minutes):

  • Complete daily commissions (4 per day) for Annulith and materials
  • Spend your daily stamina (called "Conduct") on Ascension material domains
  • Claim daily login rewards if available
  • Check in-game mail for any code or event gift deliveries

Weekly (30-45 minutes):

  • Fight weekly bosses for rare talent materials (3 boss drops per week)
  • Complete Domain challenges for Echo artifact sets
  • Push Abyssal Tower floors as far as you can (resets every 2 weeks)
  • Check the event tab for limited-time activities
  • Buy weekly refresh items from the shop using Beetle Coins

07 Frequently Asked Questions

What should I spend my first Annulith on?
The beginner banner (discounted 10-pull) is your best first spend. After that, save for limited character banners that fit your preferred playstyle.

Is NTE pay-to-win?
No. All content can be cleared with free-to-play characters and weapons. Spending mainly accelerates progression and unlocks cosmetics. The gacha system has pity mechanics that guarantee rate-up characters over time.

Does progress carry across platforms?
Yes. NTE supports full cross-platform save. Your account, characters, and progress sync between PC, iOS, and Android seamlessly after linking your account.

What is the endgame content?
The Abyssal Tower is the primary endgame challenge — a multi-floor combat gauntlet that resets periodically and rewards Annulith. Domain challenges at max difficulty also provide the best Echo artifacts for late-game optimization.

How important are gift codes?
Very important for new players. The current active codes give 380 Annulith total (nearly 4 free pulls), plus upgrade materials that save hours of early-game grinding. Always redeem all active codes before they expire.
